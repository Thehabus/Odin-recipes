Fried Calamari Rings, Italian taste!

Description

Calamari is the Italian word for squid, a cousin to octopus.
If you've done some traveling around South Italy, you likely encountered some form of fried calamari in small fish shops or taverns.
The squid is coated in batter or flour and quickly deep fried to golden perfection, then served hot with a splash of beautiful lemon slices from Sorrento or Sicily.

Ingredients

  • 3 Cups of Extravirgin Olive Oil (S. Italian one!)
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of "prezzemolo"
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 12 squid, cleaned and sliced into rings
  • 1 lemon - cut into wedges, for garnish

Steps

Just 3 strides after the prep!

  1. Preheat oil in a heavy, deep frying pan or pot. Oil should be heated to 365 degrees F (180 degrees C).
  2. In a medium size mixing bowl mix together flour, salt, oregano and black pepper. Dredge squid through flour and spice mixture.
  3. Place squid in oil for 2 to 3 minutes or until light brown. Beware of overcooking, squid will be tough if overcooked. Dry squid on paper towels. Serve with wedges of lemon.
